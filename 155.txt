The traditional media play an undoubtedly important role in the process of politicisation of EU. They remain ‘societies’ most important source of political information and the main link between institutional and public arenas. This role is important in the context of European integration project since the main political institutions and actors of the EU, compared to the national ones, are more distant from the citizens. Therefore, a thorough study of EU politicisation in the media, combined with the analysis of the parliamentary debates are crucial steps to understand how the EU politicisation shapes the EU issue voting in national elections.
