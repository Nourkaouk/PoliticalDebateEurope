What will make it even harder for Scholz and Macron to patch things up under Tusk’s influence is their divergent campaigns ahead of the European election.
“ [Scholz and Macron] are struggling to establish a working relationship, and everyday what separates them takes on a more personal edge,” Stefan Meister, researcher with the German Council on Foreign Relations, wrote in Le Monde.
French and German leaders are leading polar-opposite European election campaigns, with Scholz styling himself as “peace chancellor,” while Macron has decided to push a harder line on Russia to beat back the advance of the far-right National Rally.
