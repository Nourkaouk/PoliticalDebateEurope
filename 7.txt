The democratic legitimation of the rests on the Treaty System. The move toward unification first arose in Kellogg-Briand Pact in 1928, which gained adherent countries during negotiations and took on theme of integration for the achievement of peace between Great Powers. After Second World War, European society sought to end conflict permanently between states. In the spirit of the Marshall Plan, those two states signed the Treaty of Paris in 1951, establishing the European Coal and Steel Community.
Since then, the Treaty of Paris, which focused on price setting and competition for purposes of a common market, has been superseded.
