However, granting Corsica special status in French constitution will require the backing of both the National Assembly and the Senate, as well as a three-fifths majority in the combined chambers – making it a tall order for Macron’s minority government, which controls neither house of parliament.   
The far-right National Rally party has pledged to reject the move, accusing Macron of attempting to “deconstruct the French nation”.
