Portugal’s snap election has brought seismic change — the Socialists are in opposition and the center-right Democratic Alliance, which narrowly won the vote, will try to form a minority government. But the biggest shock came when far-right, ultra-nationalist, anti-immigrant Chega became the third-largest party in parliament.
In this episode of EU Confidential, host Sarah Wheaton is joined by POLITICO’s Aitor Hernández-Morales, Barbara Moens and Nicholas Vinocur to break down the results of the vote and discuss what the far-right surge in Portugal could mean for the EU — with the European election on the horizon and the race for top jobs underway.
