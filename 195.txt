As outlined in the IRC’s new report, there are four areas in desperate need of such new thinking.
First, we are faced with a crisis in peace-making. For example, Sudan — home to the world’s largest humanitarian crisis — is at a critical moment. The conflict and its consequences are metastasizing faster than diplomacy is working. And after the failure of successive mediation and cease-fire efforts, 25 million people are in humanitarian need, neighboring states like South Sudan and Chad are coping with hundreds of thousands of refugees, and malnutrition is rife inside the country. So, the new diplomatic muscle is essential.
