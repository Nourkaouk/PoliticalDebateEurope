This is why you get debates over really anything else and strong polarization along cultural lines. These debates often don’t lead anywhere because it becomes an ‘us versus them’ kind of politics. A political battle can never be resolved if you’re not really talking about policy.
Both culture and economics don’t explain the geographical variation in the drivers of anti-system protest. In some places, cultural factors tend to dominate. In others, the economic factors are more obvious.
So, there is an interplay between the two. Underlying economic factors can also present themselves in what some would call a cultural protest.
