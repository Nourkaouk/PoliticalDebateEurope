Nostalgia for Tusk’s previous premiership, when France, Germany and Poland could often club together to drive EU policy, may also be leading to overblown expectations of what the Polish PM can deliver.
He was seen as an effective President of the European Council between 2014 and 2019, helping move EU leaders toward agreement around the summit table in Brussels.
Even then, though, he wasn’t loved by everyone. “He was always so gloomy,” said a senior British official. “Not one you’d look to try to find a path through warring sides, so he doesn’t feel like natural fit for the task.”
