The chances of the EU taking a sharp pull to the right as a result are high. But it does make these European elections about fundamental issues, and that is good news for democracy in the EU.
Democracy in EU remains imperfect. Yet the trend of disinterest in European elections seems to have reversed: in 2019, turnout increased for the first time in 40 years. It is up to politicians and the media to do justice to this growing attention. After all, democracy in the EU thrives on open debate about political visions, not on a flat-out for or against.
