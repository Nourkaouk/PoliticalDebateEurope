When considering democratic reform in Europe, it is tempting to think there may be an ideal democratic system on which France, Germany, the UK, and others in Europe could all converge. But each country’s culture and history has created different ideas of what a good democracy is, especially when balancing the constitutional and popular pillars of liberal democracy.
But comparing democratic institutions and processes in Europe can nevertheless help better understand the strengths and weaknesses of each and therefore create a significantly more sophisticated debate about future direction and reform than if we look at each country’s system in isolation.
