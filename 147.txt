The European Union executive said it was sending its proposals to the 27 European Union member governments and the European Parliament to reflect on how to improve the position of farmers in the food supply chain.
It said the list of possible actions would be discussed by the national agriculture ministers, who are next due to convene in Brussels on March 26.
European Union Agriculture Commissioner Janusz Wojciechowski had been due to present the reforms on a visit to his native Poland earlier on Friday, but said this was delayed due to a dispute over the timing of some changes.
